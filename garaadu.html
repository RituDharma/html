“You have to come here in winter to try ratalu!!” Large chunks of the starchy purple yam,locally known as garaadu, stacked around frying pans. Sliced to order and then dunked in hot oil, ratalu is deep-fried to a uniform golden-brown and generously smothered in red chilli powder or giravan(a local spicy powder). Crisp on the surface with just the right amount of give, the ratalu will sing in your lips with its hing-heavy spice coating. Like the best winter snacks, it will also fill you with a sense of warmth and wellbeing.
